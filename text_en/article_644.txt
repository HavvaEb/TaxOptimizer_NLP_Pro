Decision of the Flemish Government concerning measures taken by the pandemic caused by COVID-19 and amending the minimum conditions for the legal status of the staff of the municipalities, the public centres for social welfare and the provinces Legal basis This decision is based on: - the Decree of 22 December 2017 on local administration, Article 195, first paragraph, and 550, first paragraph; - the Provincial Decree of 9 December 2005, Article 112, first paragraph, replaced by the Decree of 3 June 2016. Formal requirements The following formal requirements are fulfilled: - The first section of the Committee for the provincial and local administrations, Subsection Flemish Region and Flemish Community has concluded Protocol No 2020/4 of 11 December 2020. - The Treasury has given its opinion on 1 September 2020. - The Council of State has given its opinion 68.707/3 on 16 February 2021, applying Article 84, § 1, first paragraph, 2°, of the Laws on State, coordinated on 12 January 1973.In accordance with Article 234 of the Provincial Decree of 9 December 2005, 5° the staff of the public welfare centre referred to in Article 182 and 183 of the Decree of 22 December 2017; 6° the staff of the welfare association referred to in Article 488, § 1 of the Decree of 22 December 2017; 7° the staff of the autonomous care institution referred to in Article 499 of the Decree of 22 December 2017.Article 5, § 1, 5°, of the Law of 27 March 2020 authorizing the King to take measures to combat the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19 (II) on corona parental leave shall be treated as parental leave referred to in Article 136 (3) of the Decree of 7 December 2007, Article 99 (3) of the Decision of 12 November 2010 and Article 5 (1) (3) of the Decision of 13 September 2002. Article 8. § 1. If the statutory staff member, mentioned in Article 1 of the Decree of 7 December 2007 and Article 2 of the Decision of 12 November 2010, cannot telework, also not in any other appropriate function, and if there is no solution to be found by overtime or other forms of leave, that staff member shall, in the following cases, be entitled to leave of: 1° if a minor child living with him or not in his day care, or cannot go to school, because the class or school of which forms part of a measure to restrict the distribution of COVID-19, shall not be entitled to leave in the following cases:In Article 114 of the Decree of 7 December 2007 and in Article 80 of the Decree of 12 November 2010, § 3. The leave referred to in paragraph 1 shall, for the purposes of calculating the end-of-year allowance, be treated as periods for which the staff member received the full salary referred to in Article 136 (3) of the Decree of 7 December 2007 and in Article 99 (3) of the Decree of 12 November 2010, provided that the staff member was entitled to full benefits or incomplete benefits during the reference period. § 4.Article 12 The third paragraph of Article 164 of the same Decision shall be added as follows: "The Council may provide that the staff member may exchange, on a voluntary basis, the amount of the bicycle allowance referred to in paragraph 1 of this Article against advantages for the promotion of bicycle mobility. The Council may lay down the detailed rules in the legal status regime." Article 13. The third paragraph of Article 176 (1) of the same Decision shall be added as follows: "The Council may decide that holidays granted in respect of each calendar year above the minimum of twenty-eight days of annual leave shall be eligible by the staff member on a voluntary basis for advantages for the promotion of bicycle mobility. It shall lay down the detailed rules in the legal status scheme." Article 14 of the same Decision, amended by the decisions of the Flemish Government of 16 January, 23 November and 2 December 2016, shall be replaced by the following:In all three pillars of the medical/social scale within the meaning of the rules on family allowances, the maximum duration of the leave to be taken by the staff member shall be extended by two weeks at the time of the simultaneous adoption or foster care of several minor children. If only one of the cohabiting partners adopts the child or exercises the foster care, only that person shall be entitled to the leave. § 2. During the leave to be received, the staff member shall retain the right to his usual salary. The leave shall begin within two months after the child is registered as a member of the family. In the context of an international adoption, the board may also determine that the period to be taken into account for the actual reception of the adopted child in Belgium, if that period does not exceed four weeks and if it is spent on the preparation of the actual reception of the child.The maximum duration of the foster parental leave shall be doubled if the child satisfies one of the following conditions: 1° the child has a physical or mental incapacity of at least 66%; 2° the child has a condition which results in at least four points being awarded in Pillar 1 of the medical social scale within the meaning of the rules on family allowances; 3° the child has a condition which results in at least nine points being awarded in all three pillars of the medical social scale within the meaning of the rules on family allowances.In Article 140 of the same decision, as amended by the decision of the Flemish Government of 2 December 2016, point 1° shall be replaced by the following: "1° maternity leave, maternity leave, foster care leave and foster parental leave;" CHAPTER 5. - Final provisions Art. 21.
