Special power decision of the Brussels Capital Government No 2020/007 concerning aid for recognised social inclusion undertakings affected by urgent measures to limit the spread of the Coronavirus COVID-19 The Brussels Capital Government, having regard to the order of 19 March 2020 to grant special powers to the Brussels Capital Government in the context of the Health Crisis Covid-19, Article 2 (1); having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 1 April 2020; having regard to the urgent need, justified by the measures adopted by the Federal State on 18 March 2020 to strengthen the structures of the Social Economy, and which lead to the partial or complete closure of numerous undertakings, the Brussels Government decided on 19 March 2020 to take measures to support the economy and employment; that specific measures were also taken for social inclusion undertakings; that the deterioration of the economic situation.December 2013 on the application of Articles 107 and 108 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union to de minimis aid, published in the Official Journal of the European Union L352 of 24 December 2013; 5° BEW: Brussels Economy and Employment at the Regional Public Service Brussels. CHAPTER 2. - Form, amount and grant of aid Art. 2. The Minister grants aid to the social undertaking recognised by the Brussels Government in application of the Order of 23 July 2018 and is affected by the urgent measures to restrict the dissemination of the Coronavirus COVID-19, in so far as it has submitted to BEW a mandate application, in accordance with Article 2 of the Brussels Capital Government Decision of 16 May 2019 concerning the mandate and compensation of the social integration undertakings. The aid shall be granted under the conditions laid down in the Regulation. The public legal persons referred to in Article 7 (2) of the Order of 23 July 2018 shall not be eligible for the aid referred to in paragraph 3.The Minister may extend the time limit for the decision-making period if the available budget appropriations are exhausted. Article 7. In application of Article 39, second paragraph of the decision of the Brussels Capital Government of 13 July 2006 relating to administrative and budgetary control as well as budget preparation, the opinion of the Treasury Inspector shall not be required for expenditure on the organic expenditure, which in this case shall not be charged to the budget of the Regional Government of Brussels, on the basis of the following basic locations: 1° BA 16.003.3300; 2° BA 16.007.38.3132.The aid is limited to the social enterprises which: - were recognised in application of the decree of 23 July 2018 on the recognition and support of social enterprises; - and to the extent that Brussels Economy and Employment (BEW) have applied for a mandate, pursuant to Article 2 of the Brussels Capital Government Decision of 16 May 2019 on the mandate and compensation of social integration enterprises; - and have an activity of production of goods and/or services in the following domains: recreational, cultural, folkloristic activities, discotheques, cafes and restaurants, travel agencies, tour operators, booking agencies and related activities, hotels, shops and retail (except food shops, including the night food stores, animal food shops, pharmacies, newspaper shops, gas stations and fuel suppliers) and hairdressers. Undertakings that do not comply with the closure obligation, are not eligible for this premium.
