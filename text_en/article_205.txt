Law of 19 January 2010 amending the Agreement between the Kingdom of Belgium and the State of Malta on the avoidance of double taxation and on the prevention of tax evasion and the Protocol, signed in Brussels on 28 June 1974, as amended by the Additional Agreement signed in Brussels on 23 June 1993 (1) ((2)(3) FILIP, King of the Belgians, to all who are now and will be listed below, Our Greet. The Chamber of Representatives has adopted and We confirm, as follows: Article 1. This Act governs a matter as referred to in Article 74 of the Constitution. Art. 2. The Protocol, signed in Brussels on 19 January 2010, amending the Agreement between the Kingdom of Belgium and the State of Malta on the avoidance of double taxation and preventing tax evasion, and the Protocol, signed in Brussels on 28 June 1974, as amended by the Additional Agreement signed in Brussels on 23 June 1993, shall have the full effect of this Act, which shall be issued by the Belgian Government and shall be published by the Belgian Official Journal in Brussels, 11 September 2015.The competent authorities of the Contracting States shall exchange information which is not expected to be relevant for the implementation of the provisions of this Agreement or for the application or enforcement of the national legislation relating to taxes of any kind or name which are levied by the Contracting States, in so far as the taxation provided for in that legislation is not contrary to the provisions of this Agreement. The exchange of information shall not be restricted by Articles 1 and 2.If, on the basis of the provisions of this Article, information has been requested by a Contracting State, the other Contracting State shall immediately use the means available to it to obtain the information requested, even if that other State does not require that information for its own tax purposes; the obligation contained in the preceding sentence shall be subject to the restrictions provided for in paragraph 3 of this Article, but in no case shall such restrictions be interpreted as allowing a Contracting State to refuse to provide information merely because that State has no domestic interest in that information. 5. In no case may the provisions of paragraph 3 of this Article be interpreted so as to permit a Contracting State to refuse to provide such information only because the information is in the possession of a bank, another financial institution, a trust, a foundation, an agent or a person operating in the capacity of a representative or a confidence person or because the information relates to a person.IN WITNESS WHEREOF, to that end, the undersigned have signed this Protocol, in duplicate in Brussels, on 19 January 2010, in the English language.
