whereas, on a proposal from the members of the United College responsible for Assistance to Persons, Decision: Article 1. § 1, paragraph 2 (1) of the Special Powers Decision of the United College of the Joint Community Commission No 2020/02 to safeguard the operation of the public centres for social welfare in the context of the COVID-19 health crisis, shall be replaced by the following:(b) The measures of public policy adopted by the competent authorities shall, directly or indirectly, prevent the holding of meetings of the Board of Social Welfare, of the Permanent Bureau or of the Special Committee for the Social Service. The body concerned shall formally determine the inability to physically meet before the virtual meeting is opened. This finding shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting in question. The statement referred to in point (a) above shall be transmitted by electronic means to the Secretary-General. By way of derogation from Article 30, third paragraph of the Organic Law of 8 July 1976 on Public Welfare Centres, when the meeting of the Board of Social Welfare or the Fixed Bureau is held in a virtual manner, the invitation and all documents relating to the agenda items shall be communicated to the relevant members only by electronic means.The minutes shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting, duly signed by the Secretary-General. The minutes shall indicate the channel through which the virtual meeting took place. Where appropriate, the votes of each member shall be recorded in the minutes. All decisions taken at the virtual meetings of the Social Welfare Council or of the Permanent Bureau shall be fully transmitted to the United College within the time limit laid down in Article 110 (1) of the Organic Law of 8 July 1976 on Public Welfare Centres, unless these decisions relate to individual cases of social assistance. The provisions of Article 111 of the same law shall apply.' Article 6 of the same Decision shall replace the 3rd paragraph by the following: "This expenditure shall be included in the next amendment of the budget not later than three months after the end of the period of 60 days from 16 March 2020."
