Decision of the Walloon Government concerning the granting of a concession in the form of a safeguard mechanism in the context of the crisis in connection with the Coronavirus COVID-19 The Walloon Government, having regard to the Decree of 11 March 2004 on regional incentives for small and medium-sized enterprises, and in particular Articles 10 and 19 thereof; having regard to the report of 14 June 2021 drawn up in accordance with Article 3 2° of the Decree of 11 April 2014 implementing the resolutions of the United Nations Women's Conference in Beijing in September 1995 and integrating the gender dimension into the whole of regional policies; having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 16 June 2021; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 17 June 2021; having regard to the request for an opinion within a period of 5 days, addressed to the Council of State on 1 March 2021, pursuant to Article 84 (1) of the Laws of State, coordinated on 12 January 1973; having regard to the opinion of the Council of Budget, given on 17 June 2021;Having regard to Commission Regulation (EU) No 1407/2013 of 18 December 2013 on the application of Articles 107 and 108 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union to de minimis aid;The health crisis linked to the coronavirus COVID-19 is recognised by the Government as an exceptional event within the meaning of Article 10 of the Decree. Article 3. The concession is granted in accordance with Commission Regulation (EU) No 1407/2013 of 18 December 2013 on the application of Articles 107 and 108 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union to de minimis aid, hereinafter referred to as Regulation (EU) No 1407/2013. Article 4. According to the detailed rules which he had concluded, the Minister grants financial aid to the company: 1° which, before 11 May 2021 in the Walloon Region, owned an establishment unit as referred to in Article I.2., 16° of Book I of the Economic Law Code; 2° which was still closed on 1 May 2021 in the framework of a federal measure to tackle the COVID-19 outbreak; 3° which demonstrated a turnover loss of 50% in respect of the second quarter of 2019;(g) 92,000 of NACE BEL code. (h) 93,122 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,123 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,124 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,125 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,126 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,127 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,128 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,129 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,130 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,199 of NACE BEL code; (h.13) 93,211 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,212 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,129 of NACE BEL code; (h) 93,129 of NACE BEL code;In the case of undertakings operating in the business sector referred to in Nace-BEL code 56.302, the contribution referred to in Article 4 (1) shall be equal to or higher than 50 if the number of employees is 0; 2° 12,000 if the number of employees is 0 and less than 10; 3° 18,000 if the number of employees is 10 or less than 50; 4° 24,000 if the number of employees is 50 or more than 50. By way of derogation from Article 1, 7°, the number of staff, if the company is set up in 2020, shall be calculated on the average number of employees in 2020. The allowance referred to in paragraph 1 shall be granted to the undertaking on the web platform only once in accordance with book III, Title 2, Chapter 1 of the Economic Law Code.This Decision shall enter into force on the day on which it is published in the Belgian Official Journal.
