Decision of the Walloon Government amending certain provisions of the Walloon Regulatory Code of Social Action and Health concerning the financing of operators in the sector of social action in the context of the health crisis COVID-19 The Walloon Government, having regard to the Code of Social Action and Health, and in particular Articles 47 thereof, replaced by the Decree of 20 February 2014, Article 47/3, inserted by the Decree of 20 February 2014, Article 56, as amended by the Decree of 20 February 2014, Article 56/7, as amended by the Decree of 23 March 2017, Article 114, as amended by the Decree of 28 April 2016, Article 115, as amended by the Decree of 20 February 2014 and 28 April 2016, inserted by the Decree of 1 March 2018, and Article 154/4, inserted by the Decree of 27 March 2014 and amended by the Decree of 8 November 2018; Having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 5 May 2021; Having regard to the approval of the Minister of the Budget, given on 6 May 2021, having regard to the report drawn up on 23 April 2021 pursuant to the Decree of 3 March 2016, adopted by the United Nations Security Council on 27 March 2014 and amended by the Decision of 8 November 2018,the measures taken by the various levels of government, and in particular the measures taken at the end of the meeting of the Consultative Committee of 24 March 2021 to limit the spread of these crises; whereas, in view of Article 191 of the EU Treaty which lays down the precautionary principle in the context of the management of an international sanitary crisis and the active preparation for the potential occurrence of these crises; whereas this principle implies that, where there is a serious risk of all likelihood, the public authorities should take urgent and provisional measures; whereas, in view of the WHO declaration in relation to the characteristics of the coronavirus COVID-19, in particular the high level of infection and mortality risk of it, the WHO has raised to the highest degree on 11 March 2020 the Coronavirus COVID-19 as a pandemic in Belgium; whereas, on 16 March 2020, the WHO has established its level of threat to the coronavirus COVID-19, which destabilizises the world economy, to the highest degree of its activity;Whereas the measures provided for in this Regulation are in accordance with the opinion of the Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs and Industrial Policy,For the purpose of applying this Decision, the amount of the subsidy shall not be higher than the actual cost borne by the beneficiary for what is subsidised. CHAPTER II. - Measures relating to social inclusion Article 1. This Decision shall not apply, in accordance with Article 138 of the Constitution, to the matter referred to in Article 128 thereof.By way of derogation from the previous paragraph, if the service was not open between 1 March 2019 and 29 February 2020 because it had not yet been established, and therefore cannot justify activity during that period, an average of the months during which the service was open shall be taken into account. Article 6. By way of derogation from Article 38/12 of the "CRWASS' shall not be taken into account in the context of a review of the decision to recognise a service. CHAPTER V. - Measures concerning the services and arrangements for the supervision of partner violence and gender-based violence Art. 7. By way of derogation from Article 235/10 of the "CRWASS', the amount of the subsidy for the year 2022 relating to the activity of the service in 2021 and the balance of the grant for the year 2021 calculated on the basis of the number of contracts awarded shall be calculated on the basis of the amount obtained above the amount of all months of the year in accordance with Article 235/10 of the "CRWASS'.CHAPTER VII - Final provisions Art. 9. The periods and periods referred to in this Decision may be extended by the Minister for Social Action if the health conditions related to the health crisis COVID-19 persist after 1 October 2021. Article 10. This Decision shall enter into force on 1 April 2021. Names, 27 May 2021. The Prime Minister, E.
