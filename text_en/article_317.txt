Decision of the Brussels Capital Government concerning aid to the food and beverage undertakings in the context of the health crisis COVID-19 The Brussels Capital Government, having regard to the decree of 3 May 2018 concerning aid for the economic development of undertakings, Articles 28 and 30; Having regard to the decision of the Brussels Capital Government of 15 October 2020 concerning aid to the event, entertainment, tourism and cultural sector in the context of the health crisis COVID-19, Article 3; Having regard to Article 2, § 3, 5°, of the decree of 4 October 2018 introducing the equal opportunities test; having regard to the opinion of the Finance Inspector, given on 25 October 2020; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 26 October 2020; having regard to the opinion of the Economic and Social Council for the Brussels Capital Region, given on 9 November 2020; having regard to the urgency, justified by the fact that the COVID-19 health crisis has had significant economic consequences for many undertakings; having regard to the agreement of theIn accordance with Article 84, paragraph 1, paragraph 3 of the Laws of the Council of State, coordinated on 12 January 1973; On the proposal of the Minister of Economy, After deliberation, Decision: CHAPTER 1. - General provisions Article 1. For the purposes of this Decision, the following shall mean: 1° Minister: the Minister of the Brussels Capital Government responsible for Economy; 2° Federal Closure measure: Article 6 (1) of the Ministerial Decision of 18 October 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19; then Article 6 (1) of the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020 laying down urgent measures to restrict the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19, as well as their subsequent amendments; 3° De minimis Regulation: the Regulation (EU) No 1407/2013 of the Commission of 18 December 2013 on the application of Articles 107 and 108 of the Treaty on the operation of the European Union to theThe aid provided for in this Decision shall apply, without prejudice to the conditions laid down in the Order of 3 May 2018 relating to aid for the economic development of undertakings'. Article 4. The beneficiary which is sanctioned on the basis of Article 29 of the Ministerial Decision of 18 October 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19, Article 26 of the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19, or any provision which replaces it, is excluded from the aid or, where appropriate, is obliged to repay it, in accordance with Article 26 of the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020. The beneficiary shall, for a period of three years from the date on which the grant of the aid is subject to the condition laid down in paragraph 3.Article 9. § 1. The management and control of the applications may, in order to verify or supplement the data specified in the application, concern the processing of the following categories of personal data: 1° the identification and contact details of the persons submitting the application on behalf of the beneficiaries; 2° the identification, address and contact details of the independent undertakings, natural person requesting the premium; 3° the information concerning penalties and infringements of the beneficiaries referred to in Article 4. § 2. BEW is the controller for the personal data referred to in § 1. BEW may obtain the personal data and other data from the applicant or from any other public authority. § 3. The maximum retention period for personal data processed pursuant to this Article shall be three years from the date of the refusal of the application or the clearance of the aid, except for personal data which may be necessary for the treatment of disputes with the aid applicant, during the time necessary to deal with such disputes, including the execution of any judicial decisions.
