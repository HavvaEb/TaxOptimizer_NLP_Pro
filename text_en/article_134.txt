Royal Decree amending Article 2 of the Royal Decree of 9 February 2017 implementing Article 323/1 of the Income Tax Code 1992 providing for the electronic exchange of information relating to mortgage loans and individual life insurances REPORT TO THE KONING Sire, The draft Royal Decree which I have the honour to submit for signature to Your Majesty aims to amend Article 2 of the Royal Decree of 9 February 2017. This Royal Decree determines the data relating to mortgage loans concluded by their clients, which the credit institutions must provide to the Federal Government Treasury in accordance with the provisions of Article 323/1 WIB92. However, the COVID-19 crisis caused to many citizens for loss of income, leading to difficulties in the payment of mortgage loans. By means of the charters relating to the deferment of mortgage loans concluded between the Minister of Finance, the National Bank of Belgium and Feblfin, the persons financially affected by the crisis to their bank may, under certain conditions, be valid for a certain number of months, in respect of this possibility of taxing Flemish loans.From the date of expiry of the loan and any amendments thereto, which the FPS Finance already has pursuant to Article 2 of the Royal Decree of 9 February 2017. The then Commission for the Protection of Privacy has already expressed its favourable opinion on these data in its Opinion No 02/2017 of 11 January 2017. It was therefore not considered appropriate to obtain a new opinion. I have the honour to be, Sire, Van Your Majesty, the very respectful and very faithful servant, The Minister of Finance, V.
