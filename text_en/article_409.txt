Decision of the Flemish Government to regulate certain temporary measures by the civil emergency in relation to public health under the Coronavirus COVID-19, as regards the second review round of 2020 for project grants and grants within the Arts Decree Legal basis This decision is based on: - the Decree of 20 March 2020 on measures in the event of a civil emergency in relation to public health, Article 5. Formal requirements The following formal requirements are fulfilled: - The Flemish Minister, responsible for the budget, has given his agreement on 23 April 2020. - No opinion has been requested from the Council of State, applying Article 3 (1) of the Laws on the Council of State, coordinated on 12 January 1973. The urgent need does not allow to wait for the opinion of the Council of State within a shorter period of 5 days, for the sake of the corona crisis and the very rapid evolution of the situation. It is crucial for the field of arts that there is immediately clear about the second subsidy round of 2020 within the Arts Decree, with extreme if 15 May 2020. Maximum legal certainty within the shortest period is necessary.In addition, pursuant to Article 44 of the Arts Decree and Article 16, paragraph 1, of the Flemish Government's decision of 9 November 2018 concerning the implementation of the Decree of 13 December 2013 supporting the professional arts, applications for project grants within the Arts Decree are advised by an assessment committee composed of at least 3 and up to 13 members, chaired by a chairman. At this moment, in view of the applicable corona measures, it is uncertain whether such assessment committees will be able to take place in respect of the grant round with deadline of 15 May 2020. For this reason, only within the review round of 15 May 2020 will applications for project grants from individual artists and organisations be assessed in the same way as applications for grants: with an ad hoc commission consisting of 3 members of the pool of evaluators and a secretary. Therefore, the risk of unlawful composite assessment committees resulting from the failure of committee due to illness or other necessary activities as a result of corona. Small commissions can also be easily met and recommended.By way of derogation from Article 3, second paragraph, 2°, of the Flemish Government's decision of 9 November 2018 on the implementation of the Decree of 13 December 2013 on the support of professional arts, applications for subsidy shall be submitted by 15 May in the year 2020 at the latest. These applications shall relate to projects starting from 1 September to 31 December 2020. Art. 2.
