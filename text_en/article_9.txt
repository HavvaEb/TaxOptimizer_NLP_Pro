Royal Decree on the termination of Articles 3, 4 and 7 of the Royal Decree of 22 April 2020 laying down special measures for staff of the Federal Public Service in the context of the health crisis resulting from the Coronavirus COVID-19 FILIP, King of Belgians, to all who are now and will be listed below, Our Greet. Having regard to the Constitution, Articles 37 and 107, second paragraph; Having regard to the Law of 16 March 1954 on the control of certain institutions of public service, Article 11, paragraph 1, replaced by the Law of 24 December 2002; Having regard to the Law of 22 July 1993 laying down certain measures relating to civil service, Article 4, § 2, 1°, as amended by the Law of 20 May 1997; Having regard to the exemption from the regulatory impact analysis provided for in Article 8, § 1, 4°, of the Law of 15 December 2013 concerning administrative simplification; Having regard to the opinion of the Minister of Finance, given on 5 June 2020;4 of the Council of State, given on 24 June 2020 in application of Article 84, § 1, paragraph 1, paragraph 3°, of the laws on the Council of State, coordinated on 12 January 1973; to the nomination of the Minister of Civil Service and to the opinion of the Ministers meeting in Council, We have decided and decide: Article 1. Articles 3 and 4 of the Royal Decree of 22 April 2020 laying down special measures for staff of the Federal Public Office in the context of the health crisis caused by the Coronavirus COVID-19 shall be lifted. Article 2. Article 7 of the Royal Decree of 22 April 2020 laying down special measures for staff of the Federal Public Office in the context of the health crisis caused by the Coronavirus COVID-19 shall be repealed. This Decision shall enter into force on 1 July 2020, with the exception of Article 2, which shall enter into force on 1 September 2020.
