On the occasion of the 35th anniversary of the Erasmus project FILIP, King of Belgium, to all who are now and will be listed below, Our Greet. Having regard to the Law of 31 July 2017 on various financial and fiscal provisions and measures relating to concession agreements, Article 3; having regard to the Royal Decree of 3 September 1998 on the national side of the first series of euro coins, as amended by the Royal Decrees of 13 December 2007, 19 March 2009 and 26 January 2014; having regard to the Decision of the European Central Bank of 7 December 2021 on the approval of the size of the currency issue in 2022; having regard to the opinion of the Finance Inspection, given on 27 January 2022; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 21 February 2022; having regard to the laws of the State, coordinated on 12 January 1973, Article 3, replaced by the Law of 4 July 1989 and amended by the Law of 4 August 1996,§ 3. By way of derogation from the first paragraph, the national side of the second commemorative coin depicts the sculptor of Erasmus. On the right hand side is a portrait of Erasmus with the indication of the years 1987-2022 and the text "Erasmus programme." Central to the coin is the country designation "BE" and below it is the interiors of the designer Joaquin Jimenez. On the left side is the number 35 in a meshwork that symbolises the many intellectual and human exchanges between European students. Below are shown the mint master mark and the mint mark of Utrecht, the Mercuriusstaff, place where the coins are minted. Art. 3. This decision takes effect on the day on which it is published in the Belgian Official Journal. Art. 4. The Minister responsible for Finance is responsible for the implementation of this decision in Brussels, 15 March 2022.
