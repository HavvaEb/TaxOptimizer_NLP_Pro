The Decree on the granting of an additional remuneration to non-profit-making associations operating an economic activity in the context of the COVID-19 crisis (1) The Walloon Parliament adopted by the Walloon Government, and We, Walloon Government, confirm the following: Article 1. For the purposes of this Decree, the following shall mean: 1° non-profit-making association: the non-profit-making association referred to in Book 9 of the Company and Associations Code: (a) subject to VAT; (b) employing at least one person with an employment contract; (c) employing less than 250 full-time equivalents with an employment contract; (d) carrying on an economic activity, namely the provision of goods or services on a given market; (e) whose financing from public sources does not exceed 50%, with the exception of employment aid, on the basis of the approved accounts for 2019; 5° NACE-BEL code: the nomenclature of activities established by the National Institute for Statistics (NACE-BEL 2008) in a single European context, imposed by Council Regulation (EEC) No 3037/90 of 9 October 1990 concerning the statistical nomenclature of activities in the European Community, amended by Regulation No.The Convention on the operation of the European Union to de minimis aid, hereinafter referred to as Regulation 1407/2013. Article 3. In order to qualify for the additional compensation referred to in Articles 4 and 5 above, the non-profit-making association must comply with the legal provisions applicable to the pursuit of its activities and to the tax, social and environmental laws and regulations of the same kind. Article 4. The Government shall, in accordance with the detailed rules laid down by it, grant an additional remuneration to the non-profit-making association which, before 19 October 2020, owned a branch unit referred to in Article I.2, 16°, of Book I of the Code of Economic Law, had to conclude a federal or regional measure to combat the COVID-19 epidemic, which was qualified as a pandemic by the World Health Organisation on 11 March 2020, and whose activity is covered by one of the NACE BEL codes referred to in one of the following subclasses: 1° 56.101 of NACE-BEL code; 2° 56.102 of NACE-BEL code; 3° 56.301 of NACE-BEL code; 4°The Government grants an additional fee to the non-profit-making association which, before 2 November 2020, owned a branch unit as referred to in Article I.2, 16°, of Book I of the Economic Law Code, which had to conclude a federal or regional measure to combat the COVID-19 epidemic, which was classified as a pandemic by the World Health Organisation on 11 March 2020, and whose activity is covered by one of the NACE BEL codes listed in one of the sectors or sub-sectors referred to in the following NACE BEL code: 1° 47.990 of NACE BEL code as regards the provision of goods to and in-house; 2° 85.510 of NACE BEL code; 3° 55.300 of NACE BEL code; 4° 56.210 of NACE BEL code; 5° 59.140 of NACE BEL code; 6° 82.300 of NACE BEL code; 7° 85.510 of NACE BEL code; 8° 85.510 of 8°The Government may, in so far as it has been imposed by a federal or regional measure taken to combat the COVID-19 epidemic, which has been qualified by the World Health Organisation on 11 March 2020 as a pandemic. Art. 6. The fee referred to in Articles 4 and 5 may be granted only once per non-profit association registered in the Cross Point Bank of Enterprises, in accordance with Book III, Title 2, Chapter 1 of the Code of Economic Law. The fee referred to in Articles 4 and 5 shall be excluded from the non-profit association which, within the framework of the grant of the COVID-19 epidemic, which is qualified by the World Health Organisation as a pandemic on 11 March 2020, shall be granted to an amount exceeding the minimum thresholds laid down in this Decree. Art. 7.The Government shall examine whether the application for a financial concession is admissible. If the file is inadmissible, the Government shall suspend the application for a financial concession and inform the non-profit association thereof, which may complete its application and have it subject to a reexamination of the admissibility. If the file is not supplemented and subject to a new admissibility inquiry within one month of the date of suspension, the application shall be definitively rejected. If the application fulfils the conditions, the non-profit association shall be informed electronically that the financial concession is granted. Art. 9. The fees provided for in this Decree shall not be granted or refunded: 1° in the case of bankruptcy, dissolution or voluntary or judicial liquidation of the association without profit; 2° in the case of the non-profit-making provision by the association of incorrect or incomplete information on the amount of the compensation, without prejudice to the criminal prosecution of the persons who would have provided information. Art. 10. This decree shall enter into force on the day on which the Belgian Official Journal is published.Discussion. Vote.
