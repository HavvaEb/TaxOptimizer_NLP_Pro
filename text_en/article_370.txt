Royal decision to support young people and students in a vulnerable situation under the COVID-19 crisis FILIP, King of Belgium, to all who are now and will be listed below, Our Greeting. Having regard to the law of 22 December 2020 laying down the general budget for the financial year 2021, Article 2.44.2; having regard to the organic law of 8 July 1976 concerning the public centres for social welfare, Articles 1 and 57, § 1; having regard to the law of 22 May 2003 concerning the organisation of the budget and the comptability of the federal State, Articles 121 to 124; having regard to the royal decision of 16 November 1994 concerning administrative and budgetary control; having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 20 February 2021; having regard to the agreement finding of Our Minister of Budget, given on 26 February 2021, it is possible that the granting of medical, social or psychological assistance to the public welfare centres for the purposes ofThis allowance is charged on the loan entered in the general expenditure budget for the year 2021, Section 44, organizational division 55/1, basic allocation 44.55.11.43.52.01 and will be compensated through the interdepartmental commission attached to the Coronavirus COVID-19. § 3. The allowance referred to in § 1 shall be allocated to the centres in accordance with the distribution key provided for in the following paragraph. The amount of the allowance shall be distributed between the OCMWs on the basis of the average sum of the number of young people under the 25 years and students as defined by the law of 26 May 2002 on the right to social integration, which have received a living wage during the period between 1 November 2019 and 31 October 2020. The amounts shall be rounded off to units of one euro.
