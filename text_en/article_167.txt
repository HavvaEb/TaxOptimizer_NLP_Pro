The Royal Decree implementing Article 12 (10) of the Law of 2 April 2021 providing temporary support measures as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic and concerning the tax reduction for the acquisition of new shares of companies which have seen their turnover fall sharply under the COVID-19 pandemic REPORT TO THE KONING Sire, the decision submitted for signature to Your Majesty aims to implement Article 12 (10) of the Law of 2 April 2021 providing temporary support measures as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. This Article has introduced a new tax reduction for the acquisition of new shares of companies which have seen their turnover fall sharply during the 2 Gulf of the COVID-19 pandemic. Article 12 (10) of the aforementioned Law delegates to the King the power to determine the proof required for obtaining the tax reduction. Article 1 follows the schedule already drawn up by Articles 63 12/1 and 63 12/3, KB/WIB 92, the tax reduction for the acquisition of new shares of new companies (in accordance with Article 26 of the Law of 26 December 2020) to determine whether it is necessary for the purpose of obtaining the tax reduction.For the period from 2 November 2019 to 31 December 2019, or both the turnover realised for the period from 2 November 2020 to 31 December 2020 and the turnover provided for in the financial plan for the same period (in the event that the company was established after 2 November 2019, other than in the context of a merger or division of companies); - the fact that the taxable person is still in possession of the said shares on 31 December of the year of the acquisition. 2° For each of the following five years following the acquisition, the document must confirm that the taxable person is still in possession of the said shares and that the conditions laid down in Article 12 (2) of the same law are still fulfilled. 3° Where appropriate, the document for the year of the disposal of the shares or for the year in which the terms of the same law are no longer fulfilled, indicate the number of months still to be taken into account for the calculation of the reduction.from Wilfried Van Vaerenbergh, chairman of the Chamber, Jeroen Van Nieuwenhove and Koen Muylle, state councils, Jan Velaers and Bruno Peeters, assistants, and Astrid Truyens, Registrar.
