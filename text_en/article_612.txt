Decision of the Walloon Government of special powers No 26 granting, on a temporary basis, additional aid to the bridging right granted to self-employed persons in the context of the crisis of the Coronavirus COVID-19 and amending various laws and regulations The Walloon Government, having regard to the Law of 23 March 2020 amending the Law of 22 December 2016 introducing a transitional right for self-employed persons and introducing temporary measures within the framework of COVID-19 in favour of self-employed persons; Having regard to the Decree of 11 March 2004 concerning regional incentives for small and medium-sized enterprises, Article 4, first paragraph, 5°, and Article 10; Having regard to the Decree of 15 December 2011 concerning the organisation of the budget, the accounting and the reporting of the Walloon public administrations; Having regard to the Decree of 19 December 2019 on the general budget of the Walloon Region for the financial year 2020;Having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 27 April 2020; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 28 April 2020; having regard to the laws of the Council of State, coordinated on 12 January 1973, Article 3 (1) paragraph 1; having regard to the urgent need which does not allow the opinion of the Council of State's legislative department to wait within a period of five days, in particular because of the following provisions; having regard to the consultations between the Governments of the participating entities and the competent federal authorities of the National Security Council, meeting since early March 2020; having regard to Article 191 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, having regard to the decision of the precautionary principle in the context of the management of an international health crisis and the active preparation of the potential of this crisis; having regard to the principle of, where it is most likely to be for the public authorities, to take urgent and provisional measures; having regard to the statement of the precautionary principle in the light of the decision taken by the United Nations Security Council on 27 April 2020;the ministerial decision of 3 April 2020 and the ministerial decision of 17 April 2020; having regard to the urgent need to be justified by the fact that the Federal Minister of Security and Home Affairs has ordered the closure of a large number of undertakings suffering major economic damage as a result of the health crisis COVID-19; that it is necessary to provide urgent aid to the undertakings affected, in order to limit economic damage; that the undertakings affected are seeing their turnover fall significantly, or even disappear completely, by the closure measures, and that this would jeopardise the income of the entrepreneurs and their employees; that this aid is intended, inter alia, to avoid a wave of bankruptcies among the undertakings experiencing acute liquidity problems as a result of the crisis; that the aid should therefore be paid as quickly as possible; that defaults due to liquidity problems could cause a domino effect in the economy, which should be avoided at all expense; that these problems and effects will be felt at very short notice and that it would not be justified to carry out any delay in the aid; that the aid should therefore be paid out as quickly as possible; that the aid would be necessary;The financial compensation referred to in Article 10 (1) of the Law of 22 December 2016 introducing a transitional right for self-employed persons and in Article 4 (1) of the Law of 23 March 2020 amending the Law of 22 December 2016 introducing a transitional right for self-employed persons and introducing temporary measures for self-employed persons in the framework of COVID-19; 6° the additional aid: the additional aid to the right of bridging granted under the conditions laid down in this Decision. Article 2. The Minister shall, in accordance with the modalities laid down by him, grant an additional aid to the beneficiaries who have exercised their activity in the Walloon Region and who demonstrate that they have decisively interrupted their activity, whereby they have, before the date of publication of this decision in the Belgian State Gazette, submitted an application for a transitional right in favour of the company in March-April 2020.The aid referred to in Article 2 (3) and (4) shall be granted under this Decision in accordance with Commission Regulation (EU) No 1407/2013 of 18 December 2013 on the application of Articles 107 and 108 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union to de minimis aid. Article 4. According to the arrangements laid down by the Minister, beneficiaries and undertakings shall submit the application for the aid referred to in Article 2 (3) or (4) of the budget, the accounting and reporting of the Walloon public administrative units. Article 5. In accordance with the Decree of 15 December 2011 on the organisation of the budget, the accounting and reporting of the Walloon public administrative units, the accounting and reporting of the Walloon public administrative units, the administration may request any useful document which is used for the purposes of Article 21 (1) of the Decree of 15 December 2011 on the organisation of the budget and the purpose of Art.(d) in the first paragraph a point 4° /2 is inserted as follows: "4° /2 68.311 of the NACE BEL code;"; (e) in the first paragraph a point 4° /3 shall be inserted as follows: "4° /3 90 to 93 of the NACE BEL code." Article 8 of the same Decision shall be replaced as follows: "The application for compensation shall be submitted before 13 May 2020, with the exception of Article 4, 1° /1 to 4° /3 90 to 93 of the NACE BEL code."
