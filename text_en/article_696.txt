Royal Decree amending the Royal Decree No 20 of 20 July 1970 fixing the rates of value added tax and the classification of goods and services at those rates relating to mouth masks and hydroalcoholic gels REPORT TO THE KONING Sire, The measure is intended to temporarily allow the application of a reduced rate of 6 pc. to the supply, intra-Community acquisition and importation of certain protective equipment for use by anyone. Those protective equipment may but be subject to the reduced rate of VAT of 6 pc. on condition that they are necessary to prevent the spread of COOVID-19. It is intended to: - the mouth masks whose importation until 31 July 2020 is exempt from VAT by application of Commission Decision (EU) 2020/491 of 3 April 2020 granting exemption from import duties and of VAT on imports for goods necessary to combat the effects of the COVID-19 outbreak in 2020; - the hydroalcoholic gels.(e.g. wholesaler, pharmacies, retail, online trade) and regardless of the buyer's status (first-line care staff, care institutions, individuals and any other natural or legal person). This exceptional measure also applies within the strict framework of the fight against that pandemic and accordingly has a limited period of time: it will apply from 4 May 2020 to 31 December 2020. I have the honour to be Sire, Van Your Majesty, the very respectful and very faithful servant, The Vice-First Minister and Minister of Finance, A.
