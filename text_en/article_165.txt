Having regard to the special law of 8 August 1980 on the reform of the institutions and in particular Article 6 thereof; having regard to the special law of 12 January 1989 on the Brussels institutions; having regard to the decree of 19 March 2020 on the granting of special powers to the Brussels Capital Government in the context of the health crisis COVID-19; having regard to the Royal Decree of 8 March 1989 on the establishment of Leef environmental Brussels, Article 3, § 2; having regard to the decision of the Government of the Brussels Capital Region of 23 March 1994 on the management of waste arising from activities in the health care sector (hereinafter referred to as 'the Decision of the Brussels Capital Region'),Whereas, after consultation with the sector (hospitals, the federation of go4circle, SANTHEA and virologists), the measures for sorting and alternative packaging of the "COVID-19" waste have been interpreted and clarified in accordance with the Decision of 1 April 2020 in the circular of 1 April 2020; whereas, since these alternatives do not allow for the management of the increase in waste flows and only relate to "COVID-19" waste; whereas, therefore, the legal characteristics of the packaging of infectious waste must be temporarily relaxed, without compromising the environmental protection and the principles of waste management; whereas the characteristics of the flexible and hard packaging and its conditions of use must also be clarified; whereas the protection of personnel responsible for the management of this waste must be ensured at all times and must not be at the expense of the safety of the waste managers who manage this waste during its collection and treatment;Whereas, taking into account the urgency to ensure the management of waste from health care, it is appropriate, in accordance with Article 2 of the Ordinance of 19 March 2020 granting special powers to the Government, as a matter of urgency to obtain the opinion of the Council of State, to Decision: Article 1. Under Article 6 of the Decision of the Government of the Brussels Capital Region of 23 March 1994 on the management of waste from health care activities within the meaning of paragraph 3 and paragraph 4 which shall read as follows: "The waste referred to in Article 1, 2°, (a) shall not be packed into hard or semi-hard containers in accordance with the ADR. The waste referred to in Article 1, 2°, (a) shall not be packed into flexible packaging in accordance with the ADR in the case of difficulties in the supply of packaging provided for in paragraph 3 as a result of the COVID-19 health crisis, (b) shall not be used in accordance with Article 6 of this Decision.The combustion plants which, due to the COOVID-19 health crisis, are not subject to the conditions set out in paragraph 1, provided that the following cumulative conditions are met: 1. The special waste is packed in hard packaging; 2. In the interim storage a temperature of up to 15°C is maintained; 3. The storage period of the special waste in the interim storage may not exceed 5 days." Article 4. In point 1 of Annex IV to the same Decision, a member is added as follows: "In the event of difficulties in supplying packaging which makes it possible to comply with this point due to the COOVID-19 health crisis, a derogation may be made from the characteristics in terms of colour and minimum dimensions of the label."Brussels, 18 June 2020.
