Decision of the Brussels Capital Government on the compensation of non-profit-making creative and cultural institutions adopted by urgent measures to limit the spread of the COVID-19 virus The Brussels Capital Government, having regard to the decree of 3 May 2018 on aid for the economic development of undertakings, Article 28; having regard to the fact that the COVID-19 health crisis is to be regarded as an exceptional event within the meaning of Article 28 of the decree of 3 May 2018 on aid for the economic development of undertakings; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 27 May 2020; considering the urgency of the present and future measures taken to limit the spread of the virus on 18 March 2020; having regard to the further spread of the COVID-19 virus, the economic and social consequences of which affect the very short term of creative and cultural institutions,(b) the European Parliament and the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of individuals with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data and repealing Directive 95/46/EC (General Data Protection Regulation);on the repayment of such compensation by the applicant or beneficiary of: 1° to whom a penalty is imposed pursuant to Article 10 of the Ministerial Decision of 23 March 2020 laying down urgent measures to restrict the dissemination of the Coronavirus COVID-19, or any other regulation replacing it or replacing it; 2° which does not comply with all the applicable obligations of social, labour and environmental law; 3° against whom a bankruptcy or liquidation procedure has already been initiated on 18 March 2020, or who is in a state of bankruptcy or liquidation, has ceased his business, undergoes judicial reorganisation, or has declared bankruptcy, in respect of whom a liquidation or judicial reorganisation procedure has been initiated on 18 March 2020, or is in a similar situation to a similar procedure in other national regulations; 4° which deliberately provides false or incorrect information; 5° which is located in one of the cases referred to in Article 3, § 1, paragraph, of the general rules of 8 October 2015 concerning the retention, recovery and non-payment of subsidies in respect of employment and economic matters;In accordance with the decision of the Brussels Capital Government concerning aid for the event sector, the nightlife sector and the tourism and cultural sector in the context of the COVID-19 health crisis. 7° which, in the context of the COVID-19 health crisis, has applied for aid from another region, a community or an institution of public utility dependent on one of these entities and fulfils the conditions for that purpose. As from the date of the grant, the beneficiary shall comply with the conditions referred to in paragraph 1 for a period of three years. CHAPTER 3. - Procedure for examining the file with regard to applications for aid and payments Art. 5 The applicant shall submit his application for aid to BEW at the latest on 23 November 2020 and shall complete the form BW makes available on its website. On grounds of inadmissibility, the applicant shall attach the following information to his application:For the purposes of Article 8 (1) of the Law of 8 August 1983 regulating a national register of natural persons. § 2. BEW is the controller of the personal data referred to in § 1. BEW may obtain the personal data, and other data, from the applicant or from another public authority. § 3. The maximum retention period for personal data processed pursuant to this provision shall be three years, except for personal data which might be necessary for the treatment of disputes with the aid applicant, during the time necessary to deal with such disputes. CHAPTER 4. - Control and reimbursement of aid Art. 9. Regional employment inspectors of the administration referred to in Article 1, 4° of the Brussels Capital Government's decision of 9 June 2016 determining the authorities responsible for the supervision and control of employment matters and laying down further rules on the operation of these authorities shall be responsible for the supervision and control of compliance with this Decision.
