The Ministerial Decision concerning the extension of the measure of interruption of the execution of the sentence "COVID-19" in accordance with Article 66 (2) of the Law of 20 December 2020 on various temporary and structural provisions relating to justice in the context of the fight against the spread of COVID-19 COUNCIL OF STATE SECTION Legislation Opinion 69.521/1 of 11 June 2021 on a draft decision of the Minister of Justice on the extension of the measure of interruption of the sentence 'COVID-19' in implementation of Article 66 (2) of the Law of 20 December 2020 laying down various temporary and structural provisions relating to justice in the context of the fight against the spread of COVID-19'On 4 June 2021, the Council of State Department of Law was requested by the Minister of Justice to give an opinion within a period of five working days on a draft ministerial decision on the extension of the measure of the interruption of the sentence 'COVID-19' in implementation of Article 66 (2) of the Law of 20 December 2020 on the temporary and structural provisions relating to justice in respect of the jurisdiction of COVID and the jurisdiction of the Court of 20 June 2021.Coronavirus COVID-19, which is the subject of this draft decision, must, at the latest, intervene before the earliest expiry date of the ongoing penalty interruptions, since - without timely certainty as to the ministerial approval for further granting and extension - on the one hand, the ongoing penalty interruptions could otherwise be extended for a short period (with legal uncertainty in respect of the sentenced person and a major procedural burden which can be renewed thereafter) and, on the other hand, this could lead to the closure of the convicted persons concerned after the end of their individual penalty interruption, which would undermine the finality of the measure. This request for an opinion is submitted to you subject to the signature of the draft royal decision implementing Article 81 of the said law, which was also submitted to you for an opinion at the same time, and whereby the measure of interruption of the sentence would be extended until 15 October 2021 and with which this draft ministerial decision is to be read together.Article 66 (2) of the Law of 20 December 2020 contains a specific authorisation to the Minister to decide that the director may extend the decision to grant the interruption of the sentence and not to grant the interruption for the first time.to determine, for the first time in that period, that this measure may be granted for the first time in respect of the duration of this measure (taking into account that for ministerial decisions no recourse can be made to the general implementing powers). 25 JUNE 2021. - Ministerial decision on the extension of the measure of interruption of criminal execution "COVID-19" pursuant to Article 66 (2) of the Law of 20 December 2020 laying down various temporary and structural provisions on justice in the context of the fight against the spread of COVID-19 The Minister of Justice, having regard to the Law of 20 December 2020 laying down various temporary and structural provisions on justice in the context of the fight against the spread of COVID-19, Article 66 (2);
