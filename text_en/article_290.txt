Law providing for exceptional measures in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic on pensions, supplementary pensions and other social security benefits (1) FILIP, King of Belgium, to all who are now and will be listed below, Our Greet. The Chamber of Representatives has adopted and We confirm, as follows: CHAPTER 1. - Introductory provision Article 1. This Act governs a matter as referred to in Article 74 of the Constitution. CHAPTER 2. - Exceptional measures in the field of statutory pensions under COVID-19 pandemic Art. 2. This Chapter applies to the following benefits and their additional benefits: 1° the retirement and survivor's pensions provided for in Article 38 of the Law of 5 August 1978 concerning economic and budgetary reforms, and Article 80 of the Law of 3 February 2003 concerning various amendments to the legislation relating to pensions of the public sector; 2° the retirement and survivor's pensions for workers referred to in the Royal Decree No 50 of 24 October 1967 on the retirement and survivor' pensions for employed persons;In so far as such income derives from a professional activity commenced or extended in the context of the fight against the coronavirus COVID-19, and in so far as this professional activity is exercised in one of the companies of the crucial sectors or in the essential services, as set out in the Annex to the Ministerial Decision of 23 March 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19, Article 4. § 1. For the scheme of cumulation of the benefits referred to in Article 2, 1° to 3°, with a replacement income, no account shall be taken of the following benefits granted to the beneficiary of the benefits referred to in Article 2, 1° to 3°, or to his spouse: 1° the benefit for temporary unemployment due to force majeure, including each supplement granted therein, in so far as it relates to the period from 1 March 2020 and the unemployment due to the benefit of the coronavirus COVID-19;§ 1. For the arrangement of the cumulation of the benefits referred to in Article 2, 1° to 3°, with a fee, shall not take into account the following allowances granted to the beneficiary of the benefits referred to in Article 2, 1° to 3°, or to his spouse: 1° the income compensation payments granted by the regions to undertakings for the economic consequences they are having as a result of the application of the ministerial decisions of 13 March 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19 and of 18 March 2020 urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19; 2° the flat-rate compensation granted to the temporarily unemployed, referred to in Article 6 (1) of the Decree of 3 April 2020 derogating from various provisions of the Energy Decree of 8 May 2009, the Decree of 18 July 2003 concerning the integral water policy, coordinated on 15 June 2018, and its implementing decisions, and to cover the costs of electricity consumption, heating or water consumption for the first month of temporary unemployment resulting from the corona crisis; 3° the compensation granted for the non-reside days of 24 May 2009,on condition that they are of the same nature and in so far as they relate to the period from 1 March 2020 and have been granted on account of the coronavirus COVID-19. Art. 6. The period referred to in Articles 3, 4 and 5 starting from 1 March 2020 shall not end on 30 June 2020. The King may, for the provisions of Articles 3, 4 and 5 which he designates, extend the period referred to in paragraph 1 of this Article. Article 7. This Chapter shall have effect with effect from 1 March 2020.
