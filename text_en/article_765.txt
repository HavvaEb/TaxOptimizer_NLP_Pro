Decision of the Brussels Capital Government concerning aid to the undertakings of the tourist accommodation sector in the context of the health crisis COVID-19 The Brussels Capital Government, having regard to the decree of 3 May 2018 concerning aid for the economic development of undertakings, Articles 28 and 30; Having regard to Article 2 (3), 5° of the Order of 4 October 2018 introducing the equal opportunities test; having regard to the opinion of the Economic and Social Council for the Brussels Capital Region, given on 22 March 2021; Having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 12 April 2021; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 14 April 2021; having regard to the opinion of the Economic and Social Council for the Brussels Capital Region, given on 22 April 2021; having regard to the urgency, motivated by the fact that the COVID-19 health crisis has significant economic consequences for many undertakings; having this context seriously reduced the turnover of the undertakings of the tourist accommodation sector; having regard to the Commission's opinion of the Economic and Social Council for the Brussels Capital Region, given on 22 April 2021;the economy, in view of the current CoviD-19 outbreak, as amended by the communications of 3 April 2020, 8 May 2020, 29 June 2020, 13 October 2020 and 28 January 2021; 3° KBO: the Cross Point Bank of Enterprises; 4° W: Brussels Economy and Employment of the Regional Public Service Brussels. Art. 2. The Minister grants support to the undertakings of the tourist accommodation sector for the loss of income suffered, in the context of the health crisis COVID-19. The health crisis COVID-19 is recognised as an exceptional event as referred to in Article 28 of the Order of 3 May 2018 on aid for the economic development of undertakings. The aid is granted under the conditions referred to in paragraph 22 of the Temporary Framework on State aid. The aid conditions laid down in this Decision shall apply without prejudice to the conditions laid down in the Order of 3 May 2018 on aid for the economic development of undertakings.The first paragraph shall not apply to the beneficiaries registered in the KBO as from 1 January 2019. Art. 5. § 1. If the beneficiary is a company, unless the beneficiary is between the end of the financial year and the day of the aid application has re-assessed his own funds so that it is greater than the half of the financial year in question: 1° the own funds included in the accounting code 10/15 plus the code 101, is greater than the half of the subscribed capital, code 100, plus the issuing premiums, code 11, unless the beneficiary is between the end of the financial year in question and the day of the aid application has re-assembled its own funds so that it is greater than the amount of the capital in question.For the first paragraph, the conditions laid down in paragraph 1 shall be determined on the basis of a certificate issued by a certified auditor in respect of 2018 and 2019, supported by the relevant fragments of Part 2 of the declaration and the tax bill of the passenger tax for the tax year 2020 (revenue 2019). If the beneficiary has not achieved a turnover from a tourist accommodation activity in 2018, he shall comply with the condition laid down in paragraph 1, 2°. § 3. Paragraphs 1 and 2 shall not apply to the beneficiaries who have been registered in the KBO for less than three years at the date of entry into force of this Decision. Art. 6 shall apply only to the data entered in the KBO at the date of application of the aid application. CHAPTER 3. - Form and size of the aid Art. 7 shall consist of a premium per active establishment unit in the Region registered in accordance with Article 3, 3°, the starting date of which shall be 1 January 2021, from: 1° 12,500 euro, if the number of full-time equivalents is less than 5,The beneficiary shall notify the beneficiary of the additional documents and information within 10 days. If no reply is received within this period, the application shall be refused. Article 10. The grant decision shall be served on the beneficiary within the 12 months following the granting of the necessary information on any aid of more than EUR 100,000 granted on the basis of the extended State aid website or the IT instrument of the European Commission.Paragraph 3 Without prejudice to Article 12, the maximum retention period for personal data processed pursuant to this Article shall be 10 years from the date of settlement of the aid, except for personal data which might be necessary for the handling of disputes with the aid applicant, which shall be kept for the time necessary to deal with such disputes. CHAPTER 5. - Final provisions Article 14. This Decision shall enter into force on 24 June 2021. Article 15. The Minister shall be responsible for the implementation of this Decision. Brussels, 21 June 2021. For the Brussels Capital Government: The Prime Minister of the Brussels Capital Government, responsible for Tourism, R.
