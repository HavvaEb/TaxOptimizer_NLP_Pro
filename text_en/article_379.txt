Decision of the Walloon Government of special powers No 35 organizing the 'contact tracing' in the context of the fight against the COVID-19 epidemic The Walloon Government, having regard to the special law of 8 August 1980 on the reform of the institutions, as amended by the special law of 6 January 2014, Article 5 (1), (1) and (8); having regard to Articles 47-13 to 47-16 of the Walloon Social Action and Health Code, inserted by the Decree of 2 May 2019; having regard to the special decree of 11 July 2014 on the powers of the French Community to which the exercise is transferred to the Walloon Region and to the French Community Committee, Article 3, 6°; having regard to the decree of 17 March 2020 granting the special powers to the Walloon Government in the context of the health crisis COVID-19 for matters governed by Article 138 of the Constitution; having regard to the decision of the Walloon Government of 13 September 2019 establishing the distribution of the ministerial powers and the settlement of its instruments; having regard to the decision of the Walloon Government of 26 September 2019 on the basis of theHaving regard to the lockdown measures taken by the Federal Government through the successive ministerial decisions of 13, 18 and 23 March 2020 and of 3, 17 and 30 April 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19, published respectively in the Belgian Official Journal of 13, 18 and 23 March 2020, and of 3, 17 and 30 April 2020; having regard to the decision adopted by the National Security Council on 24 April to initiate, from 4 May 2020, a process of gradual reduction of the lockdown in successive phases; whereas this process of phasing out the lockdown has the effect of increasing contacts between people and consequently leading to an increased risk of contamination with COVID-19; whereas Article 191 of the EU Treaty establishing the precautionary principle lays down in the management of an international sanitary crisis and the active preparation of these crises; whereas this principle implies that, where there is a serious risk of serious danger, it is necessary for the public authorities to adopt provisional measures with regard to such persons;Whereas, in the case of infectious diseases, there are currently procedures for the protection of public health and the application of appropriate prophylactic measures contained in the Walloon Code of Social Action and Health, including the procedures for "tracing"; whereas those necessary specific modalities, namely the establishment of a contact centre, are thus added to the modalities already provided for in Articles 47/13 and following of the Walloon Code of Social Action and Health; whereas, for COVID-19 specific modalities are different from certain modalities of the tracing provided for in the Walloon Code of Social Action and Health, which is justified in view of the scale of the epidemic and the need to take coherent and coordinated measures between sub-regions and federal authorities throughout the Belgian territory; whereas the "Agence wallonne de la santé, de la protection sociale, du handicap et des families" (Wals Agency for Health, Disability and Family) with theWhereas Article 2 (1) of the Decree of 17 March 2020 granting special powers to the Walloon Government in the context of the health crisis Covid-19 provides that: "In order to enable the Walloon Government to react to the pandemic Covid-19, the Government may, in matters falling within the competence of the Walloon Region under Article 138 of the Constitution, take all appropriate measures to prevent and deal with any situation which raises problems within the strict framework of the pandemic Covid-19 and which is to be governed by the penalty of serious danger"; whereas Article 1 of the Decree of 18 December 1992 provides that "the decision of the Walloon Region may be adopted by the Council in accordance with Article 138 of the Constitution, taking all appropriate measures to prevent and deal with any difficulties in the strict framework of the pandemic Covid-19 and its consequences of serious danger."By way of derogation from Article 47/14, all members of the contact centre may collect data by means of other means not expressly mentioned in Article 47/14, paragraph 7 of the Code such as home visits to persons who are not available by phone or e-mail.Contract pursuant to Article 28 of Regulation (EU) No 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of individuals with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data and repealing Directive 95/46/EC.
