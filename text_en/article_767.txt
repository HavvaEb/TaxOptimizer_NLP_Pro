Royal Decree No 32 on urgent measures to restrict the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19 in respect of certain postal services FILIP, King of Belgium, to all who are now and will be listed below, Our Greeting. Having regard to the Law of 27 March 2020, which authorises the King to take measures to combat the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19 (II), Articles 2 and 5, § 2; Having regard to the Law of 26 January 2018 on postal services, Articles 3, 4, 16 and 25; Having regard to the Royal Decree of 24 April 2014 regulating the postal service; Having regard to Article 46 of the Code of Justice; Having regard to the Law of 15 December 2013 laying down various provisions on administrative simplification, Article 8 § 2, ° 1 and 2°; having regard to the opinion of the inspector of Finance, given on 14 May 2020; Having regard to the agreement of the Minister of the Budget of 29 May 2020; Having regard to the opinion of the Council of State, given on 11 June 2020, given to Article 8 (2) and 2° 1 of the Law of 27 March 2020,§ 4. By way of derogation from Article 15 of the Royal Decree of 24 April 2014 regulating the postal service, registered postal consignments shall be issued without endorsement to the person empowered to receive them. A verification of the identity of the recipient shall be carried out, in accordance with the procedures laid down in Article 8 (1) of the Royal Decree cited above. Article 2. By way of derogation from Article 46, paragraph 1, second paragraph of the Court Code, the receipts of the letters of formal notice submitted shall not be signed and dated. The postal services shall notify this impossibility by the words "COVID-19 procedure" at the bottom of the receipt certificate or by electronic application in the case of an electronic receipt certificate. Article 3. This decision shall have effect with effect from 13 March 2020 and shall apply until 31 August 2020. The King may, by decision adopted in the Council, amend the final date provided for in paragraph 1.
