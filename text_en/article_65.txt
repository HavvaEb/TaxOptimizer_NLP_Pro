Decision of the Brussels Capital Government concerning aid to undertakings engaged in a contact or non-essential activity in the context of the health crisis COVID-19 The Brussels Capital Government, having regard to the order of 3 May 2018 concerning aid for the economic development of undertakings, Articles 28 and 30; Having regard to Article 2, § 3, 5°, of the Order of 4 October 2018 introducing the equal opportunities test; having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 1 June 2021; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 3 June 2021; having regard to the opinion of the Economic and Social Council for the Brussels Capital Region, given on 16 June 2021; having regard to the opinion of the Data Protection Authority, given on 14 June 2021; having regard to the urgency, motivated by the fact that the COVID-19 health crisis has significant economic consequences for many undertakings; that the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020 imposing urgent measures to restrict the dissemination of the coronavirus COVID-19, as amended by the Decision of 28 June 2021; having regard to the decision of 14 June 2021; having regard to the decision of the decision of the Data Protection Authority, given on 14 June 2021; having regard to the opinion of theThe Commission's Regulation (EU) No 1407/2013 of 18 December 2013 on the application of Articles 107 and 108 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union to de minimis aid, published in the Official Journal of the European Union L352 of 24 December 2013;The Brussels Capital Government of 12 October 2020 on the basis of the aid granted to the creative and cultural institutions without a profit made by the urgent measures to limit the spread of the COVID-19 virus shall, on the basis of Article 12 of the Financial Regulation of 12 October 2020 on the basis of which it is registered in the CBO before 1 January 2019, declare, on the honour that its loss of turnover over the first four months of 2021 in relation to the first four months of 2019 is at least equal to the amount of aid provided for in Article 8 or 10, as the case may be. Article 4. The beneficiary has achieved in 2019 a turnover exceeding EUR 25,000. The turnover shall be determined on the basis of the data entered in the VAT returns receipts of the VAT unit of not later than 24 June 2021.(d) the activity '96,092 - Places of tatouages and piercings';The beneficiary may not combine the various premiums referred to in Chapters 3 and 4 of this Decision with the other aid applications of the same kind, except in respect of the two premiums he applies for. BEW receives the aid application at the latest on 28 July 2021. In his application, the beneficiary shall indicate the other aid covered by the de minimis regulation or other de minimis regulations which the undertaking has received during the two preceding tax years and the current tax year. BEW may request by e-mail all documents or information which it considers necessary for the examination of the application. The beneficiary shall provide the additional documents and information within 20 days. If no reply is received within this period, the aid shall be refused. The grant decision shall be served within two months of the date of receipt of the application to the beneficiary.- The Minister of the Brussels Capital Government responsible for Economics, A.
