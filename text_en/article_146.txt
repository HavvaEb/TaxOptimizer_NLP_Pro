Decision of the Walloon Government of 27 November 2004 concerning several measures to address the effects of the COVID-19 crisis on employment, education and participation, including the social economy The Walloon Government, Having regard to the Act of 28 December 1944 concerning social security for workers; Having regard to the Walloon Code of Social Action and Health; Having regard to the Decree of 10 April 2003 concerning the training of workers employed by an undertaking; Having regard to the Decree of 11 March 2004 concerning the recognition and subsidisation of regional employment services; Having regard to the Decree of 27 May 2004 concerning the advisory agencies on social economy; Having regard to the decision of 14 December 2006 concerning the recognition and subsidisation of the "Initiatives de développement de l'emploi dans le secteur des services à final sociale" (Initiatives for the development of employment in the community services sector with a social purpose),Having regard to the urgent need to combat the COVID-19 epidemic, in particular to Walloon employment, socio-professional integration and social economy policies; whereas the crisis may free the sectors and public employment schemes, social inclusion and social economy, as well as the objectives pursued by these schemes; whereas the measures envisaged are indispensable to ensure employment in these sectors and the maintenance of social performance resulting from these schemes; whereas it is appropriate to exempt the grants granted and to ensure the rights of beneficiaries within the framework of the employment, social inclusion and social economy policies; whereas the measures envisaged are indispensable to ensure employment in these sectors and the maintenance of social performance resulting from these schemes;In view of the request for advice within a period of five days, addressed to the Council of State on 4 June 2021, pursuant to Article 84 (1), first paragraph, 3°, of the laws of the Council of State, coordinated on 12 January 1973; having regard to the lack of advice within that period; having regard to Article 84 (4), second paragraph, of the laws of the Council of State, coordinated on 12 January 1973; having regard to opinion No 21/07 of the Management Committee of Forem, given on 24 June 2021; having regard to opinion No 1469 of the "Conseil économique et social de Wallonie" (Social-Economic Council of Wallonia), given on 14 June 2021; having regard to the proposal of the Minister of Work and Formation; having regard to Decision No 1469 of the General provisions of Article 1.By way of derogation from Article 79 (1) of the Royal Decree of 25 November 1991 laying down the rules on unemployment, Article 4 shall apply from 1 July 2021 as the long-term unemployed person who may be active in a PWA the person who fulfils the following combined conditions: 1° for at least 12 months registered as a non-working job seeker with the "Office wallon de la formation professionnelle et de l'emploi" (Walish Vocational Training and Employment Service), abbreviated "FOREM"; 2° benefit from an unemployment, employment or protection allowance or benefit from a living wage or financial assistance Art. 5. By way of derogation from Article 79a (6) and (7) of the Walloon Government's decision of 27 May 2009 to implement the Decree of 11 March 2021, the maximum hours of activity which a PWA worker may perform, shall be fixed at 70 hours per month.By way of derogation from Article 9, paragraph 2, paragraph 4, paragraph 7, and paragraph 4, paragraph 1 of the same decision, the time limits for the payment of the discs of the financial incentive shall be postponed for a period equal to the period during which the beneficiary of the financial incentive has temporarily interrupted the exercise of his activities between 1 April 2021 and 31 December 2021 as a result of the COVID-19 epidemic.CHAPTER VIII. - Measure concerning training Schèque Art. 11. By way of derogation from Article 6 of the Walloon Government Decision implementing the Decree of 11 October 2007 implementing the Decree of 10 April 2003 on financial incentives for the training of employees employed by an undertaking, the training courses previously recognized by the Government may be given at a distance between 1 April and 31 December 2021. CHAPTER IX. - Final provisions Art. 12. The Minister responsible for Employment and Training is responsible for implementing this Decision. Names, 8 July 2021.
