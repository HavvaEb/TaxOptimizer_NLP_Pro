Decision of special powers of the United College of the Joint Community Commission n° 2020/006 to organise health contact research in the context of the fight against the COOVID-19 pandemic The United College of the Joint Community Committee, having regard to the order of 19 March 2020 to grant special powers to the United College of the Joint Community Committee in the context of the health crisis Covid-19, Article 2 (1); having regard to the agreement of the members of the United College, responsible for Finance and the Budget, given on 18 June 2020; having regard to the statement of the WHO on the characteristics of the COVID-19 coronavirus, given on 20 May 2020 in application of Article 84, paragraph 1, paragraph 3 of the Laws of the State, coordinated on 12 January 1973;Whereas it is necessary for this purpose to collect information on persons who have been tested positively on COVID-19 or from whom the doctor suspects such an infection, but also on persons with whom they have been in contact; whereas it is also advisable for the Joint Community Committee, within the framework of its powers in the field of preventive medicine, to recall, as soon as possible, in the context of its duties in the field of preventive medicine, the obligations imposed on persons responsible for its implementation; whereas in the context of the Joint Commission on Preventive Health Policy of 19 July 2007 concerning the prevention of health policy, and in particular the provisions of Articles 10, 12 and 14 of Council Regulation (EEC) No 1408/71, to take into account, in so far as is necessary, the personal data used in the context of the health contact research, the obligations imposed on persons responsible for the implementation of this Decision shall be exercised as soon as possible;In connection with the processing of personal data and concerning the free movement of such data and repealing Directive 95/46/EC (General Data Protection Regulation), 4° collectivity: a school, a workplace, an asylum centre, a prison, a health and housing centre, an institution for disabled persons, a crèche, a rehabilitation centre, a psychiatric hospital, etc.; 5° database: the database referred to in Royal Decree No 18 of 4 May 2020 establishing a database at Sciensano in the context of the fight against the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19.The contact centre shall collect and process the personal data of the persons referred to in paragraph 1 in the case of: 1° the persons with whom they have come into contact in the two days preceding the occurrence of the symptoms reported by them, the date of the consultation or the test referred to in paragraph 1, as well as in the following days, and shall provide them, by electronic mail or telephone, with appropriate recommendations on the basis of the information which these persons communicate; 2° the collections with which they have come into contact, so that they may take useful preventive or screening measures; such data shall be collected in the database, with the persons referred to in paragraph 1, or directly with the persons or collections with whom they have come into contact. Art. 6. § 1. When the contact centre collects personal data and processes them for the purpose referred to in Article 5, § 1, it shall collect the following personal data: 1° the identification number referred to in Article 8, § 1° or 2° of the law of 15 January 1990 establishing and organization of the social security; 2° the surname;Article 5 of the Convention on the protection of personal data and the protection of personal data in the context of the fight against the dissemination of the coronavirus COVID-19. Art. 9. The Joint Community Commission shall be responsible for the processing of personal data by the liaison centre at the end of the Convention on the protection of personal data in accordance with Article 28 of the AVG. Art. 10. Unless otherwise specified, the personal data shall be collected and processed at the latest five days after the date of publication of the decision of the United College in which the Joint Community Committee on the protection of personal data at the end of the Convention on the protection of personal data referred to in Article 4 of the Convention on the protection of personal data referred to in Article 4 of the Convention on the protection of personal data shall be transmitted to the Member States.Brussels, 18 June 2020. For the United College, Members of the College responsible for Health and Well-being, A. MARON E. VAN DEN BRANDT
