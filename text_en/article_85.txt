Royal decision introducing a subsidy "COVID-19" for the target group of public social welfare centres FILIP, King of Belgium, to all who are now and will be listed below, Our Greeting. Having regard to the law of 27 March 2020 authorizing the King to take measures to combat the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19 (II), more specifically Articles 2 and 5, § 1, 3° "; having regard to the organic law of 8 July 1976 concerning the public social welfare centres, Articles 1 and 57, § 1; having regard to the financial law of 20 December 2019 for the financial year 2020, Article 17; having regard to the law of 22 May 2003 concerning the organisation of the budget and the comptability of the State, Articles 121 to 124; having regard to the royal decision of 16 November 1994 concerning administrative and budgetary control; having regard to the opinion of the Finance Inspector, given on 29 April 2020; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of the budget, given on 8 May 2020; having regard to the agreement of theIn addition, Article 2. The centres shall be granted an allowance in order to allow them to provide physical, social, medical, social or psychological services to users who have lost part of their income under COVID-19 and who are no longer able to cope with the daily costs or costs associated with medical care. They may use this allowance for: 1° the provision of assistance for housing, including the charges other than the rent guarantee; 2° the provision of aid for energy consumption; 3° the provision of aid for digital support with a view to promoting the online start-up of procedures, social contacts and education support; 4° the provision of psychosocial assistance to cover the costs of recognized professional aid providers in case of domestic violence, anxiety problems and psychiatric distress; 5° the granting of aid in the field of health; 6° the granting of aid in case of unpaid invoices as a result of a reduction of resources; 7° the granting of other primary needs.This report shall be transmitted to the State through the web application "Unique Report'. The original supporting documents shall remain available for the purpose of verifying the use of the grant. § 3. The amounts not used or not accounted for shall be refunded to the State by 1 November 2021. Article 6. The grant period of this Decision shall run from 1 April 2020 to 31 December 2020. Article 7. This decision shall have effect from 1 April 2020. Article 8. The Minister for Social Integration shall be responsible for the implementation of this Decision. Dated in Brussels, 13 May 2020. FILIP Van Koningswege : The Minister of Centralstand, Self-employed, SMEs, Agriculture and Social Integration, D.
