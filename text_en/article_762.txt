Decision of the Brussels Capital Government concerning aid to undertakings engaged in non-essential activities in the context of the health crisis COVID-19 The Brussels Capital Government, having regard to the order of 3 May 2018 concerning aid for the economic development of undertakings, Articles 28 and 30; Having regard to Article 2 (3) and (5) of the Order of 4 October 2018 introducing the equal opportunities test; having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 2 February 2021; having regard to the agreement of the Minister of Budget, given on 4 February 2021; having regard to the opinion of the Economic and Social Council for the Brussels Capital Region, given on 12 February 2021; having regard to the urgency, justified by the fact that the COOVID-19 health crisis has significant economic consequences for many undertakings; having regard to the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020 laying down urgent measures to restrict the dissemination of the coronavirus COVID-19, as applied on 2 November 2020, imposed a large number of undertakings; having regard to the fact that the turnover of these establishments must continue to be subject to theThe Commission of 18 December 2013 on the application of Articles 107 and 108 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union to de minimis aid, published in the Official Journal of the European Union L352 of 24 December 2013; 4° BW: Brussels Economy and Employment of the Regional Public Service Brussels. Article 2. The Minister grants aid to undertakings which had to close one or more establishments in the Region pursuant to Article 8 of the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020 as applicable on 2 November 2020, for their income losses and fixed operating costs, in the context of the health crisis COVID-19. The health crisis COVID-19 is recognised as an exceptional event, as referred to in Article 28 of the Order of 3 May 2018 on aid for the economic development of undertakings. The aid is granted under the conditions provided for in the De minimis Regulation.the premium provided for in the decision of the Brussels Capital Government of 10 December 2020 concerning aid for the reimbursement of operators of taxi services and services for the rental of vehicles with drivers which were affected by the measures taken to limit the spread of the COVID-19 virus does not benefit from the aid granted to operators of taxi services and services for the rental of vehicles with drivers; only the information entered in the Cross Point Bank of Enterprises at the date of application of the aid application shall apply. The aid conditions laid down in this decision shall apply without prejudice to the conditions laid down in the decree of 3 May 2018 concerning aid for the economic development of undertakings. Article 4. The beneficiary shall be subject to the condition laid down in the first paragraph after a period of three years from the date of granting of the aid.§ 3. The loss of turnover shall be determined on the basis of the receipts of the VAT returns of no later than 20 February 2021, for the fourth quarter of 2020 in comparison to the fourth quarter of 2019. § 4. Fixed costs shall mean the following costs linked to the units of establishment situated in the Region during the period of their compulsory closure: 1° the rents relating to the buildings used by the beneficiary; 2° the costs related to the consumption of water, electricity, gas, telephony and internet borne by the beneficiary; 3° the repayments of mortgage loans borne by the beneficiary; 4° the premiums granted by the beneficiary in the context of its activity; 5° the social, employee and employer contributions; 6° the depreciation of investments made by the beneficiary shall be at least EUR 1.000.Article 10 (1) The management and control of applications may, in order to verify or supplement the data specified in the application, concern the processing of the following categories of personal data: 1° the identification and contact details of the persons submitting the application on behalf of the beneficiaries; 2° the identification, address, contact, turnover and fixed cost data of the independent undertakings natural person applying for the premium; 3° the data relating to penalties and infringements of the personal data of the beneficiaries natural persons referred to in Article 4.Of motor vehicles 45,320 -
