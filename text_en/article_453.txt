Decision of the Flemish Government amending Article 3 and Article 4 of the Flemish Government's Decision of 8 January 2021 implementing Article 34/1, second paragraph, and Article 47/1 of the Decree of 21 November 2003 on preventive health policy and amending the Decree of the Flemish Government of 12 June 2020 implementing the Decree of 29 May 2020 on the organisation of reporting and contact investigations under COVID-19 Legal bases This Decision is based on: -the Decree of 21 November 2003 on preventive health policy, Article 47/1, § 2, third and fifth paragraphs, and § 3, third paragraph inserted by the Decree of 18 December 2020.3° movements within the framework of urgent legal or financial matters and parental authority, with support; 4° movements within the framework of urgent and necessary care for (house) animals, if no one else can take care of this; 5° movements within the framework of a funeral; 2° after the fourth paragraph, a fifth and a sixth paragraphs shall be added, which shall read as follows: "By way of derogation from the second paragraph, the period of temporary isolation referred to in Article 47/1, Paragraph 2, paragraph 1, shall be temporarily lifted for: 1° pupils, students and trainees within the framework of an examination or a compulsory task; 2° health workers, health and professional workers in the care of the elderly; this exception may be exercised only by personnel required to ensure a minimum of basic care in the hospital staff shall be taken in consultation with the hospital management and hygiene services of the hospital.The persons listed in this paragraph shall not be subject to the following conditions: 1° in a general sense; 4° in contact with the public to the strict minimum; 5° where possible do not use public transport; 6° not be able to telework; 7° in respect of distance rules and always wear (surgical) oral mask in the appropriate manner at the workplace; 8° restriction of contact with other employees: in practice this means as much as possible separate in- and exit rooms, separate break-and-dining rooms. By way of derogation from the second and third paragraph of the temporary derogation, the term of the temporary exemption, in accordance with the first paragraph of Article 47 (1) shall be stated in accordance with the following conditions:(d) the continuity of the essential service of the company is threatened in the short term (period of quarantine); (e) all other alternatives have been considered and are insufficient; The employer shall provide to the Committee on Prevention and Protection of the Persons concerned a list of the persons mentioned in this paragraph. After approval by the Committee on Prevention and Protection at the Work of the Company the exception to the quarantine shall be verified and a nominative list of the persons concerned shall be kept on a daily basis. The persons mentioned in this paragraph shall meet the following conditions: 1° in general, have no symptoms; 2° have no high risk contact with a person who has been confirmed to the Work of the Company under the same roof;The following sentence shall be added to the second paragraph of Article 47 (1) of the Decree of 21 November 2003 or to the obligation to report to a COVID-19 test centre, to a triage centre or to their doctor mentioned in Article 47 (1) (2) of the same decree, shall not apply to persons in whom the risk of contagion due to their behaviour in a high risk area is low.' 3° the third paragraph, as amended by the decision of the Flemish Government of 29 January 2021, shall be replaced by the following: "The essential reasons set out in Article 47/1, paragraph 2, paragraph 4, paragraph 3° of the above decree of 21 November 2003 for which the temporary isolation referred to in Article 47 (1) and paragraph 2, paragraph 1 of the Decree of 21 November 2003 do not apply to persons who travel in this capacity;in the framework of cross-border co-parenting; 5° pupils, students and trainees moving abroad daily or weekly in the context of their studies or a cross-border internship; 6° cross-border students travelling in the context of compulsory education or in the context of higher education and adult education to and from the location where they receive the education; 7° The "Border Force Officers" of the United Kingdom." Article 3. This decision will enter into force on the day following its publication in the Belgian Official Journal. Art. 4. The Flemish Minister responsible for health and residential care is responsible for the implementation of this decision. Brussels, 2 April 2021. The Prime Minister of the Flemish Government, J.
