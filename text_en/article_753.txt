Decision of the Minister-President of the Brussels-Capital Region extending the Decision of 26 October 2020 laying down additional measures in addition to those laid down by the Minister of Interior to restrict the dissemination of the coronavirus COVID-19 The Minister-President of the Brussels-Capital Region, Having regard to Article 166 (2) of the Constitution; Having regard to Article 48 of the Special Law of 12 January 1989 on the Brussels institutions, as amended by the Law of 6 January 2014; Having regard to Article 11 of the Law of 5 August 1992 on the Police Office, as replaced by the Law of 7 December 1998; Having regard to Article 128 of the Province Law; Having regard to Article 4, § 2quater, 1° of the Law of 26 July 1971 on the organisation of the agglomerations and the federations of municipalities, as amended by the Act of 6 January 2014; Having regard to the Ministerial Decision of 13 March 2020 on the promulgation of the federal phase concerning the coordination and management of the crisis coronavirus COVID-19; having regard to the opinion of the opinion of 28 July 1971 on theHaving regard to the meeting of the Regional Security Council of 20 April 2021; having regard to the prior consultation with the National Crisis Centre and the Corona Commissioner appointed by the Federal State for the management of the COOVID-19 crisis; having regard to the precautionary principle in the context of the management of an international health crisis; considering the urgency and health risk associated with the development and spread of the COOVID-19 coronavirus to the population in the territory of the Brussels Region; whereas since 13 October 2020 the whole country has remained at alert level 4 (very high alertness); whereas the evaluation of the epidemiological situation carried out by the Risk Assessment Group (hereinafter RAG) shows that the number of corona infections found in Belgium is still low; whereas the number of tests carried out has also declined since 13 October 2020; whereas the number of tests carried out has been reduced; whereas the rate of reproduction - d.i.(ICU) in the Brussels region (and in the Province of Hainaut) is the highest in the country (156 COVID patients at ICU on a total of 941 patients throughout the country); this means that 58% of the total number of recognized ICU beds in the Brussels region is taken by COVID patients (which is also much higher than the national percentage of 47%); whereas, however, the percentage of positive tests in Brussels is still high (10%); whereas, together with the very high number of ICU beds adopted by COVID patients in a densely urban area such as the Brussels Capital Region, this rate is still high in force in Brussels; whereas, for the country, a more stringent reduction in the number of ICU beds adopted by COVID patients in a particularly high number of cases, the percentage of which is currently in force in Brussels should be extended to at least 7 May;Whereas Article 27 (1) second paragraph of the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19 provides that "when the mayor or the governor is informed by the health entity of the Federated Entity concerned of a local increase in the epidemic in its territory, or when it finds that it is necessary to take additional measures by the situation"; whereas the local authorities therefore retain the possibility, in addition to the measures already adopted by the Federal Government, to adopt additional measures in order to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19; whereas it is therefore necessary for the Minister of Home Affairs, who has already recalled the possibility, in the light of its Ministerial Decision of 26 March 2021 to amend the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020, to retain in the form of an urgent measure to restrict the dissemination of the coronavirus COVID-19;
