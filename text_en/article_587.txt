The Flemish Government's decision amending the Flemish Government's decision of 23 April 2021 granting a grant to local authorities to strengthen the source detection (for the period from 1 April 2021 to 30 June 2021) and the contact investigation (for the period from 1 April 2021 to 31 May 2021) to combat the COOVID-19 pandemic Laws This decision is based on: - the special law of 8 August 1980 on institutional reform, Article 20, as amended by the special law of 16 July 1993 and Article 87 (1); - the law of 16 May 2003 laying down the general provisions applicable to the budgets, the control of the grants and the accounts of the communities and the regions, as well as the organisation of the audit by the Court of Auditors, Article 3, third paragraph; - the Flemish Codex Public Finance of 29 March 2019, Article 71 to 77; - the decree of 18 December 2020 on the general budget of the Flemish Community for the financial year 2021, Article 12; - the decision of Flemish Codex Overfinance of 17 May 2019, Article 29 to 31 and Article 43 of the Flemish Government of 28 April 2021April 2021 to 31 May 2021) for the purpose of combating the COOVID-19 pandemic. The functioning of the provisions in the above Decision will be extended until 31 August 2021. - By decision of the Flemish Government of 13 November 2020 granting a grant to local authorities to strengthen the contact and source detection to combat COOVID-19 pandemic - the legal contours of the local source detection and contact research were laid down; - the contours of a specific grant to the Flemish municipalities in support of the additional use in prevention, sensitization, source detection, quarantine coaching and contact research were laid down. - the decision of the Flemish Government of 13 November 2020 to grant a subsidy to local authorities to strengthen contact and source detection to combat the COOVID-19 pandemic for the limited period from November 2020 to March 2021.The need to be able to detect rapidly the continuing movement of the local authorities in the previous months has demonstrated their added value in the additional contact of index patients, the additional detection of HRC, the full deployment of source detection, the isolation of (outbreak) clusters, the continued awareness of the follow-up of the essential basic measures and the follow-up of citizens who are placed in isolation or quarantine. Therefore, the financing model established by the Flemish Government's Decision of 13 November 2020 and extended by the above-mentioned decision of the Flemish Government of 23 April 2021 should be renewed. - This Decision aims to compensate for the costs incurred by local authorities for the establishment and exploitation of infrastructure for local contact and source detection to combat the COVID-19 pandemic.
