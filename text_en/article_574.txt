In accordance with Article 5 (4) of the Royal Decree of 30 September 2020 establishing a temporary financial intervention of compulsory medical care in the costs of special protection measures and materials, within the framework of the COVID-19 pandemic The Minister for Social Affairs and Public Health, having regard to Royal Decree No 20 of 13 May 2020 laying down temporary measures to combat the COVID-19 pandemic and to ensure continuity of care in the compulsory medical care insurance, Articles 71 and 72; having regard to the Royal Decree of 30 September 2020 establishing a temporary financial intervention of the compulsory medical care insurance in the costs of special protection measures and materials, within the framework of the COVID-19 pandemic, Article 5 (4) of the Royal Decree of 13 May 2020 establishing temporary measures to combat the COVID-19 pandemic and to ensure the security of medical care in the case, Article 2, second paragraph; having regard to the opinion of the Inspector of Finance, given on 22 January 2021; having regard to the second paragraph of Article 5 of the first paragraph of Article 11 of the S.a pharmacy opened to the public by the approved tarifing service as referred to in Article 165 (1) of the Law on compulsory medical care and benefits coordinated on 14 July 1994; for the period from 4 May 2020 to 31 August 2020, the approved tarifing service shall, on the last day of the month following the month of publication of this Decision, send a paper invoice to the Auxiliary Cash for sickness and invalidity insurance (HZIV) referred to in Article 2 (i) of the same Act, together with the overall amount for all the pharmacies affiliated to that tarifing service to the public. The HZIV shall, upon receipt of the invoice, pay the total amount referred to therein to the approved tarifing service.Article 2.
