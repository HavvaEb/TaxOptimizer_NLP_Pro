The Flemish Government's decision to amend the Flemish Government's decision of 13 November 2020 to regulate a general and selective subsidy for the organisers of childcare and out-of-school care and measures for the families in childcare and out-of-school care following the effects of the COVID-19 virus, as regards the extension of the grant Legal grounds This decision is based on: - the Decree of 30 April 2004 establishing the internal self-employed agency with legal personality Growing up, Article 5, § 2, 2°, a), inserted by the Decree of 1 March 2019, and Article 12, as amended by the Decrees of 1 March 2019 and 3 May 2019; - the Decree of 20 April 2012 concerning the organisation of childcare of babies and young children, Article 10, 3°, and Article 12, § 1, second paragraph, and § 3, as amended by the Decree of 15 July 2016.The number of infections and hospitalizations is in a declining line. However, it is also established that children from 0 to 12 years old, the target group of the sectors of childcare and out-of-school care, in the months of July and August 2021 (yet) will not be vaccinated. Distribution of the virus within that target group will therefore continue to take place. In those situations will continue to be partial or complete closure of a (childer) shelter in the event of an outbreak. Such closure has direct consequences for the income and therefore the viability of the organisers. In order to ensure the viability of the organisers facing it, it is also necessary that the subsidy provided by the Flemish Government's decision of 13 November 2020 continues to apply in the summer months, so that at least the selective compensation can be granted to close for Covid-19 purposes.
