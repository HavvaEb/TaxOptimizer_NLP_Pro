The Walloon Government's decision to extend until 31 December 2021 the provisions of the Walloon Government's decision of 30 June 2020 laying down various provisions on civil service in the context of the coronavirus pandemic and the step-by-step reconstruction of activities The Walloon Government, having regard to the special law of 8 August 1980 reforming the institutions, and in particular Article 87 (3) thereof, replaced by the special law of 8 August 1988 and amended by the special law of 6 January 2014; Having regard to the Walloon Government's decision of 18 December 2003 concerning the Walloon Civil Service Code, and in particular Articles 361 and following thereof, as amended; Having regard to the Walloon Government's decision of 13 September 2019 on teleworking, as amended by the Walloon Government's decision of 31 January 2013, for its still applicable provisions; Having regard to the Walloon Government's decision of 5 December 2019 concerning teleworking; Having regard to the decision of 13 September 2019 determining the distribution of the ministerial powers and regulating the signature of its instruments, as amended;Having regard to the Generic Guide to Combat the Dissemination of COVID-19 at Work, which is the result of the cooperation between the High Council for Prevention and Protection at Work, the FOD Employment and the Economic Risk Management Group and which is available on the FOD Employment website; having regard to the various decisions of the Consultation Committee and subsequent ministerial decisions requiring telework until 30 June 2021 and recommended from 1 July 2021; whereas since the entry into force of the Ministerial Decision of 25 August 2021 amending the Ministerial Decision of 28 October 2020 laying down urgent measures to limit the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19, there is no longer an obligation or recommendation to telework; whereas the approval of teleworking is normally granted for a maximum of 2 days per week for staff of grade A5, B1, C1 and D1; Considering the need to ensure the continuity of public service, the compliance with the health requirements and the non-discriminatoryness of each other;
